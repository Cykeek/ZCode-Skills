## Role Summary

> **VP of Engineering** — Owns engineering strategy, execution, culture, and P&L for the entire engineering organization (200-5000+ engineers). Reports to CTO/CEO. C-suite peer. Defines 5-10 year technology vision. Owns $50M-$1B+ budget. Leads M&A technical due diligence. Represents engineering to board/investors. At SVP/EVP level: global multi-geo org, enterprise-wide technology strategy, public company communications, industry standards leadership.

---

## Core Responsibilities (ATS-Keyword-Rich Bullet Bank)

### Executive Leadership & Business Strategy (All Levels)
- Define **5-10 year technology vision** aligned with business strategy; identify **competitive moats** via technology; drive **market differentiation**
- Partner with **C-suite** on **business strategy**: revenue growth, product strategy, go-to-market, competitive positioning, market expansion, customer success
- Prepare and deliver **board presentations**: technology strategy, risk posture, investment thesis, competitive landscape, talent capability, innovation pipeline
- Manage **investor relations**: technical due diligence for fundraising; analyst briefings; technology narrative for public markets; earnings support
- Lead **M&A strategy**: acquisition target identification; **technical due diligence leadership**; integration planning; synergy realization; team retention
- Drive **industry leadership**: standards bodies (CNCF Governing Board, W3C TAG, IEEE); conference keynotes; patent portfolio; academic advisory; open source governance

### Organizational Strategy & Talent Leadership (All Levels)
- Design **global organization**: 500-5000+ engineers; geographic hub strategy; time zone optimization; center of excellence; remote-first; legal entity structure
- Build **leadership bench**: Director/VP hiring & coaching; high-potential identification; stretch assignments; executive education; **succession planning** (CEO/CTO/VP pipeline)
- Own **talent strategy**: employer brand; university partnerships; diversity pipeline; compensation philosophy; equity strategy; retention programs; internal mobility
- Engineer **culture at scale**: values operationalization; recognition systems; learning culture; psychological safety; innovation time; hackathons; mobility
- Lead **change management**: transformation design; communication strategy; resistance management; feedback loops; iteration; celebration; sustainability
- Manage **performance at scale**: calibration; promotion process; compensation planning; equity refresh; terminal level management; fairness audits

### Financial Stewardship & Capital Allocation (All Levels)
- Own **engineering P&L** ($50M-$1B+): headcount (salary/equity/benefits), contractors, **cloud commit** ($20M+), tools, licenses, facilities, capital equipment
- Drive **capital efficiency**: ROI on platform investments; developer productivity; cost per deploy; cost per incident; revenue per engineer; **unit economics**
- Lead **cloud economics**: multi-cloud strategy; commitment planning (RI/Savings Plans/CUDs); spot/preemptible; **FinOps culture**; showback/chargeback; anomaly detection
- Manage **vendor portfolio**: strategic partnerships (AWS/GCP/Azure enterprise); contract negotiation; consolidation; SLA management; exit strategies; TCO optimization
- Rationalize **tooling**: license optimization; build vs buy; open source adoption; internal platform vs vendor; **TCO analysis**; renewal calendar management
- Model **M&A financials**: acquisition cost; integration cost; synergy validation; earnout structures; retention packages; budget impact

### Technology Strategy & Architecture Governance (All Levels)
- Define **platform strategy**: platform as product; self-serve capabilities; API strategy; developer portal; adoption metrics; NPS; internal customer focus
- Establish **architecture governance**: principles; patterns; standards; exception process; **technical debt portfolio**; risk taxonomy; blast radius analysis
- Manage **technical risk portfolio**: risk identification; assessment; mitigation; monitoring; board reporting; insurance; business continuity; disaster recovery
- Drive **innovation management**: R&D allocation (70/20/10); emerging tech evaluation (AI/ML, quantum, edge); academic partnerships; **IP strategy**; patents
- Execute **build vs buy framework**: evaluation criteria; vendor assessment; TCO; time-to-value; strategic control; lock-in risk; exit criteria
- Own **security & compliance**: security posture; zero trust; compliance frameworks (SOC2, ISO27001, FedRAMP, HIPAA); audit readiness; privacy engineering

### Engineering Excellence & Developer Experience (All Levels)
- Champion **Developer Experience (DevEx)**: time-to-first-commit; cognitive load; feedback loops; tooling; documentation; onboarding; satisfaction (NPS/eNPS)
- Drive **engineering productivity**: DORA metrics (lead time, deploy frequency, CFR, MTTR); SPACE framework; flow metrics; value stream mapping; bottlenecks
- Institutionalize **quality & reliability**: SLO/SLI culture; error budgets; incident management; blameless postmortems; chaos engineering; resilience patterns
- Optimize **delivery excellence**: planning cadence; prioritization frameworks; dependency management; release management; feature flags; canary deployments
- Build **data-driven engineering**: instrumentation; observability; metrics culture; experimentation; A/B testing; feature analytics; decision velocity
- Define **open source strategy**: contribution policy; project incubation; governance; compliance (license scanning); community building; upstream first
