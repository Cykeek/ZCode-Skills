## Role Summary

> **Director of Engineering** — Sets technical direction and builds the engineering organization for a major domain or the entire company (40-300+ engineers). Owns 3-year technology roadmap, organizational design, engineering budget ($10M-$200M+), and executive partnerships. Translates business strategy into technical execution. At Senior Director level, owns cross-cutting platform strategy and represents engineering to board/investors.

---

## Core Responsibilities (ATS-Keyword-Rich Bullet Bank)

### Engineering Strategy & Technology Roadmap (All Levels)
- Define **3-5 year technology vision** aligned with business strategy; identify **competitive differentiation** through technical capabilities
- Build **technology roadmap**: platform investments, build vs buy decisions, vendor evaluation, technical debt portfolio, innovation allocation (70/20/10)
- Establish **architecture governance**: **architecture review board**, principles, patterns, standards, exception process, **technical debt classification** and paydown strategy
- Manage **technical risk portfolio**: risk identification, blast radius analysis, single points of failure, mitigation investment, monitoring
- Drive **innovation management**: R&D allocation, proof-of-concepts, emerging tech evaluation (AI/ML, WebAssembly, edge), academic partnerships

### Organizational Design & Talent Leadership (All Levels)
- Design **organization using Team Topologies**: stream-aligned, platform, enabling, complicated-subsystem; **Conway's Law**, cognitive load, team APIs
- Define **span of control**: 6-8 ICs per EM, 4-6 EMs per Director; management layers, reporting lines, matrix vs functional, dotted-line clarity
- Own **career ladders**: **dual-track** (IC/Manager), level definitions, competencies, promotion criteria, terminal levels, **Staff+ expectations**
- Set **hiring bar**: interview process design, rubric calibration, **bar raiser program**, diversity sourcing, university/bootcamp pipelines
- Execute **succession planning**: leadership bench, high-potential identification, stretch assignments, executive coaching, external search

### Financial Management & Vendor Strategy (All Levels)
- Own **engineering budget** ($10M-$200M+): headcount (salary, equity, benefits), contractors, **cloud commit** (RI/Savings Plans), tools, licenses, facilities
- Lead **headcount planning**: quarterly/annual, attrition modeling, capacity buffers, critical role prioritization, contractor conversion analysis
- Manage **vendor relationships**: contract negotiation, renewal cycles, consolidation, SLA management, strategic partnerships, exit strategies
- Implement **FinOps**: commitment planning, anomaly detection, **showback/chargeback**, unit economics (cost per deploy, per incident, revenue per engineer)
- Measure **ROI**: platform investment return, developer productivity gains, cost per deploy, cost per incident, revenue per engineer

### Executive Partnership & Business Alignment (All Levels)
- Partner with **C-suite**: business strategy, OKR setting, resource allocation, trade-off decisions, risk appetite, culture alignment
- Communicate to **board/investors**: technical strategy, risk posture, investment thesis, competitive landscape, team capability, key metrics
- Collaborate with **Product/Design**: quarterly/annual planning, discovery, prioritization frameworks, technical feasibility, dependency management
- Align with **Go-to-Market**: sales engineering, customer escalations, professional services, partnership integrations, pricing/packaging input
- Lead **M&A technical due diligence**: architecture assessment, team evaluation, tech debt quantification, integration planning, synergy validation

### Culture, Developer Experience & External Brand (All Levels)
- Define **engineering culture**: values, recognition, learning culture, blameless postmortems, innovation time, hackathons, internal mobility
- Champion **Developer Experience (DevEx)**: **platform as product**, adoption metrics, **NPS**, time-to-first-commit, cognitive load reduction, feedback loops
- Lead **change management**: reorg communication, process rollout, resistance management, feedback channels, iteration, celebration
- Advance **DEI**: hiring pipeline, retention, promotion equity, pay equity, ERGs, inclusive practices, bias training, accountability
- Build **external brand**: tech blog, open source, conference speaking, university partnerships, meetups, thought leadership, awards

### Crisis Leadership & Organizational Resilience (Senior Director+)
- Command **major incidents** (SEV-0/1): cross-functional coordination, executive communication, customer communication, war room leadership
- Lead **security incident response**: breach containment, forensics, notification (legal/regulatory/customers), remediation, post-incident hardening
- Manage **regulatory response**: audit preparation, evidence collection, compliance gaps, remediation tracking, regulator engagement
- Navigate **vendor/cloud failures**: multi-cloud failover, provider outage response, contract enforcement, alternative provider activation
- Steer **organizational crises**: layoffs, restructuring, leadership transitions, morale recovery, trust rebuilding, transparency
